01 — About

A number-first mind, a plain-language mission.

My relationship with finance didn't start in a classroom — it started at my mother's desk, filling in her Excel sheets and tracking SEC stock updates on HDFC before I was old enough to fully understand what I was looking at. That early exposure, paired with a genuine love for math, turned into a real fascination with how money moves, how risk is priced, and how decisions get made when the numbers get complicated.

Today I'm exploring that fascination through code — using Python, AI, and data to build things that put financial understanding within reach of people who've never had access to it. I haven't locked in investment banking or private equity yet, but I know I want a career where I'm reading the fine print for a living, and helping others understand it too.

"I want to keep building initiatives that take something complicated and make it usable — for someone who's never had the chance to understand it before."

04 — Projects

Featured work.

AI Fellowship Project

Financial Document Simplifier

A multilingual tool that takes complex financial documents — bank statements, credit and loan paperwork — and turns them into plain, understandable language. It flags risky terms and explains them clearly, built specifically to help rural and peri-urban women navigate financial documents that are usually written far above accessible reading levels.

Built using OCR to read scanned documents, NLP and tokenization to process and simplify the language, and deployed as a working Streamlit app. It's live and functional — and it shaped how I think about my future: I want to keep building initiatives like this that use technology to solve real, human problems.
